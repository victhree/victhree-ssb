Test Two · Lesson 1

SRT — Situation Reaction Test

The Mechanics

A booklet of 60 real-world scenarios, 30 minutes to respond — roughly 30 seconds per situation. The time is deliberately short so you answer with your first honest thought, not a manufactured one. You write 1–2 lines of concrete action per situation.

Quantity vs quality — settle it now

Students obsess over "must I attempt all 60?" The honest answer: officers say "quality matters, quantity doesn't." Recommended candidates have cleared it attempting all 60 with short, obvious answers — and others have cleared attempting fewer with sharper ones. A sane target is 45–55 meaningful responses. The disqualifier is never a low count — it's vague, unrealistic, self-serving answers: solving an accident single-handedly, or grabbing personal advantage in a group problem.

The core engine — Assess → Decide → Act

StepThe officer-like version
AssessIdentify the actual core problem, not the surface drama. Most SRTs bury the real issue under a distraction.
DecideChoose using available resources, never fantasy powers. Logical decisions with what's actually at hand — no lone-hero rescues.
ActWrite a concrete verb, not an intention. "Informs the warden and arranges transport" beats "would try to help."
The filter behind every SRT

The assessor is quietly asking one question of each answer — did you prioritise self, others, or the mission? Officer-like judgment consistently protects the mission and the group over the self.
