The five dilemma structures

SRTs are engineered around a small set of conflict structures. If you learn to recognise the structure, you respond in seconds instead of freezing.

Emergency  1. Emergency / Crisis

  • Stakes: time-critical, physical. e.g. "You witness a road accident." / "A fire breaks out in a crowded area."
  • Tested: composure, presence of mind, sequencing under pressure.
  • SOP: stabilise → get help → prevent escalation. Mobilise and delegate; never solo-heroics.

Ethical  2. Ethical Dilemma: Duty vs Loyalty / Self

  • e.g. "A colleague is involved in unethical practices." / "You see a senior officer taking a bribe."
  • Tested: integrity without recklessness.
  • SOP: act on principle through the proper channel. The mature answer reports or confronts appropriately; it neither ignores nor grandstands. (Full deep-dive →)

Interpersonal  3. Interpersonal / Social Conflict

  • e.g. "A friend borrows money and doesn't repay."
  • Tested: tact, social adjustment, solving the problem while preserving the relationship.
  • SOP: address it directly but without humiliating the other party.

Team  4. Team / Leadership Under Strain

  • e.g. "Your team is struggling to meet a deadline."
  • Tested: cooperation, influence, organising ability.
  • SOP: diagnose → redistribute → lead from within the team, not above it.

Personal  5. Personal / Competing Priorities

  • e.g. "A family emergency on the day of an important meeting."
  • Tested: whether you can hold responsibility on both fronts rather than abandoning one.
  • SOP: prioritise honestly → delegate or arrange coverage → close the loop. (Full deep-dive →)
