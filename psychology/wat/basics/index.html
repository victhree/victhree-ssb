Test One · Lesson 1

WAT — Word Association Test

The Mechanics
  • 60 words, each on screen for 15 seconds, no gap between words — about 15 minutes total.
  • You write the first thought that comes to mind as one short, meaningful sentence.

Why the gun-to-head timing?

It's designed to break down your conscious filters. WAT has Jungian roots — the belief that the unconscious steers the conscious will. That's exactly why it's the least fakeable test in the battery. Use that: build the reflex, and the test works for you.

A note the assessor reads that you cannot see

A hesitation, a scratched-out word, or a skipped item is itself data — a disturbance in an otherwise smooth flow flags an emotionally loaded spot. The remedy is never "write faster." It's a mind that has already rehearsed how it meets difficulty, so the pen keeps moving.

The fatal error — one miss is fine, the pile-up is fatal

The single most destructive mistake in WAT is trying to finish word 14's sentence while word 15 is already flashing — it triggers panic and ruins the next five responses. Treat a blank as a strategic loss, not a wound: one miss is invisible among 60; a cascade of ruined responses is a visible pattern of poor recovery.

SOP — When You Blank On A Word
  1. If nothing arrives in about 4 seconds, stop. Draw a small dash and move to the next word immediately.
  2. Don't look back. Recovery is the officer-like quality tested here — moving on outscores a forced, late sentence.
  3. Reset your breathing on the next word. Protect the flow, not the individual line.
