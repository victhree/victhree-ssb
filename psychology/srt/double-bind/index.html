The double-bind trap

The hardest SRTs are deliberately built as double binds — situations that hand you two options where either choice makes you look wrong. Choose family over the meeting and you look unreliable; choose the meeting and you look cold. The design intent is that every response inside the two given options loses.

Definition

A double bind is a dilemma offering two mutually conflicting demands, such that succeeding at one means failing the other — you appear wrong no matter which you pick. The trap is accepting that only two options exist.

The escape — the signature move

The resolution to a double bind is to step outside the frame — reject the false binary and find the third path. Whenever a situation feels like "damned either way," that feeling is the signal to widen the frame, not to pick the lesser evil.

Trapped inside the binary

"I would go to my family and skip the meeting."

Reads as: abandons one duty entirely.

Steps outside the frame

"Briefs his deputy to run the meeting with clear notes, leaves for the emergency, and checks in remotely."

Shows: delegation + responsibility on both axes + planning + composure.

That single reframed response demonstrates four OLQs at once — precisely because you refused the trap of choosing one loss.
