Test One · Lesson 3

Easy words are where you over-perform

Positive, easy words — Courage, Success, Mother, Victory — are where candidates lose marks by trying too hard. The trap is writing over-dramatic, artificially patriotic sentences. Train restraint: the calmest, most matter-of-fact line on an easy word signals a settled, secure personality far better than fireworks do.

Over-performing

Success — "I will achieve grand success and make my nation proud before the whole world."

Settled & mature

Success — "Success follows consistent, honest effort."

Formatting SOPs worth drilling
  • Keep spelling clean and handwriting legible — an unreadable response cannot be assessed.
  • Drop articles (a / an / the) to shorten sentences under time pressure.
  • One clear idea per word. No compound clauses when the clock is running.

How to actually train it — build the reflex, not a sentence bank

The mistake students make is collecting good sentences. A memorised bank is exactly what the timer exposes — retrieved answers feel retrieved. Train the mind instead:

The Daily Drill
  1. Take 15 random words each day. Set a 15-second timer per word and write.
  2. For each, pause and connect it to a real experience that shows emotional strength. The reflection is the real work — you're building the reflex that produces sentences, not the sentences.
  3. Read widely and think logically in daily life. Those who habitually think positively perform better naturally, without a script.
  4. Ban the sentence bank. If a response feels retrieved rather than felt, flag it and redo it.
